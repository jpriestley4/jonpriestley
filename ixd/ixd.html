Project Overview

The Brief

Design an interactive system which enhances the experience of visitors at The Cartoon Museum by allowing visitors to leave a digital trace or engage in an act of kindness towards the space.

The Space

The Cartoon Museum in London showcases the best of British cartoon, comic art, caricature and animation through a programme of exhibitions, events and workshops.

As a design space, The Cartoon Museum has a unique subject which presents plentiful opportunities to design something truly tailored to the space.

User Research

Goals

  • To learn about the visitors of the Cartoon Museum (who they are, what they think and feel).
  • To discover what, if any, digital technology visitors use in the space.
  • To discover what opportunities there are for visitors to leave a digital trace.

Data Collection Method

  • Direct observations to collect behavioural data.
  • Interviews to collect attitudinal data.
  • Number of users/visitors: 6.

Empathy Maps

Empathy maps were created to identify what each visitor thought, felt, did and said about their experience.

These individual empathy maps were then compiled into two aggregate empathy maps to represent two user groups with separate goals:

  1. Users that visit the museum to be entertained.
  2. Users that wish to learn about cartooning.

User Personas

Personas were created to personify the two user groups, which were used as a design tool throughout the project to guide user-centred design decisions.

The design of the personas was inspired by the article, 'Make Your Personas Great Again in 7 Simple Steps', which explains how personas can be a simple and effective tool when done right.

User Journeys

User journeys were created to illustrate the needs, wants, feelings, frustrations and opportunities at every touchpoint along the paths that each user group takes to achieve their respective goals.

Design

POV Statements

Point Of View statements were created to help bridge the gap between user research and conceptual design by matching user insights to design ideas.

After reviewing the POV statements, the design challenge became clear.

The Design Challenge

How might we design an interactive system which allows users to:

  • Draw and be entertained.
  • Learn about cartooning (including the history) and its techniques.

Conceptual Design

During this divergent thinking phase of the design, the following techniques were used where the focus was on quantity over quality.

  • Brainstorming
  • ‘How Might We’ questions
  • ‘Worst Possible’ ideas
  • Sketching
  • Group discussions
  • Storyboarding

After this ideation process, convergent thinking helped to narrow down the potential ideas to a single design concept, The Cartoon Booth.

Detailed Design

Initially, sketches were made to get quick, rough and ready representations of our ideas before solidifying these sketches in the form of low-fi wireframes.

Leaving a Digital Trace: For any of the drawing activities, users can share their work and leave a digital trace in the museum. When users share to the Gallery Screen, their work appears in the digital gallery for passersby to view.

Prototype

The detailed design was delivered in the form of a high fidelity prototype.

To meet the design challenge effectively, the user can choose to complete the following 3 tasks:

  1. Draw a headline: Choose a news headline and draw your own satirical cartoon.
  2. Learn to draw: Follow a step by step guide on how to draw cartoons.
  3. Learn about cartoons: Learn about the history and styles of cartoons.

Due to constraints on time and resources, only the 'Draw a headline' task and a selection of drawing utensils were integrated into the prototype.

Evaluation

A small-scale usability test was conducted using 5 participants to evaluate the prototype. The goal of the evaluation was to identify any usability issues through formative evaluation. The participants were given a task to draw a headline and share the work once they are finished.

The usability testing was extremely helpful and highlighted a number of usability issues. A three point severity rating scale was used to help prioritise the most important issues. Those were

  • Unclear task description on the Home page
  • The undo/redo buttons not using standard placement on the interface (i.e. inconsistent with other systems)
  • The layers feature on the toolbar was unlabeled and unclear
  • The use of flags but not labels on the language page
  • Lack of clarify as to what sharing to the digital gallery entailed

The prototype was updated to fix these issues.
