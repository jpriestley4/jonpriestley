Project Overview

Brief

Research

Interviews

To understand the important concepts of the domain, the needs of the target users and commonly used terminology, I interviewed three amateur stock investors:

45 year old man who invests to prop up his pension.

27 year old man who invests in companies that he’s passionate about.

25 year old woman who invests to achieve financial independence.

To ensure a more holistic representation of the domain, I also interviewed a professional stock market investor.

Competitor Analysis

To identify standard terminology and to check if there were any missing concepts from the interviews, I reviewed similar investing forum sites (Motley Fool, Seeking Alpha and Reddit).

Domain Model

To help bridge the gap between the identified requirements and the design, I created a domain model.

The hub-and-spoke structure illustrates the key relationships between the users as the domain’s central theme and the relevant surrounding entities.

Detailed annotations are provided which give clear explanations for any industry standard jargon.

Site Map

I created a site map to illustrate the information organisation and classification schemes.

Organisation Structure

A catalogue organisation structure is used to allow users to perform both top-down and bottom-up approaches to information seeking:

  • A top-down approach begins with users selecting top-level research on an industry or country before drilling down into the related stocks or ETFs.
  • A bottom-up approach begins with users filtering for a specific stock or fund and then working upwards by discovering research relating to the company's industry or country.

Click image to view

Classification Schemes

To support the discovery of related research, a hybrid classification scheme is used whereby different classification schemes are used at different levels of the site's taxonomy.

A polyhierarchy is used at the lowest levels of the ‘Country’ and ‘Industry’ hierarchies to make it easy for users to find content that belongs to both categories.

The labelling of the site map was tested using an open card sort with 15 participants with varying levels of skill and mental models. With this insight, two top-level labels were modified, ‘Security’ (previously ‘Investments’) and ‘Guide’ (previously ‘Community’) to ensure ‘user-oriented language’.

User Journey

A user journey was created to help figure out how elements of the site will flow together.

The descriptive scenario was carefully selected in order to show all potential bottom-up and top-down browsing paths, the scoped search capability and faceted navigation.

To provide a more comprehensive view of the site, the journey also shows how a user interacts with the utility navigation through liking a stock.

Key Navigation Elements

Global Navigation: An inverted-L global navigation design (D1: S1) is used to provide a constant visual reference of related pages, supporting recognition over recall.

Breadcrumbs: To support ‘wayfinding’ and orientation, breadcrumbs (D1: S2) are used to show a user’s current location in a chosen hierarchy.

Pagination: Pagination (D3: L3) is supported on pages where there are too many items to show on one screen whilst scrolling (D1: L4) is supported on pages with content that has a linear flow, such as articles.

Associated Navigation: To support the discovery of investable securities, a list of related stocks and ETFs (D2: A2) that share a similar list of metadata tags is displayed on the security profile page. Furthermore a list of related country, industry and security articles (D2: A5) helps the user find related articles as they progressively build their own investment rationale.

Social Navigation: Community tagging (D1: SO1) allows content creators to tag their research articles using a list of centrally defined tags.

Evaluation

Five target users were recruited to evaluate the wireframes.

The most significant issue identified was that novice investors did not understand some of the terminology shown on the stock profile page, such as P/E Ratio. Therefore, help icons (D2: EV1) were added to reveal a pop-up window providing a short clarification along with a hyperlink that directs the user to a learning page under the ‘Guide’ section of the site for further explanation.

Looking forward, web analytics can shed some light on the behaviour of users visiting the site. This analysis will stimulate further user research to help improve the design of the site’s information architecture.
